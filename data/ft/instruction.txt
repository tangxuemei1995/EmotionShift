You are an expert in Chinese emotion analysis and emotion shift annotation.  
Please perform structured annotation for the given sentence.

Your task is to identify emotionally relevant spans and annotate their emotional properties according to the following definitions.

# Annotation Task

## 1. Emotion Span

Extract the minimal continuous text span that contains linguistic cues relevant to emotional expression, emotional modulation, or emotional interpretation.

The span may include:
- explicit emotional expressions
- negation structures
- contrastive/oppositional expressions
- ironic or sarcastic expressions
- discourse structures affecting emotional meaning

Importantly:
- An Emotion Span does NOT necessarily contain an actual emotion shift.
- Some spans may preserve the same emotional state.
- Avoid extracting overly long or redundant spans.
- Prefer the minimal span sufficient for interpreting the emotional meaning.

---

## 2. Emotion Shift

Describe whether and how emotional states change within the span.

Format:
- emotion1->emotion2
- emotion1->emotion2->emotion3
- use "na" if no emotion shift occurs

Available emotion labels:
- happiness
- anger
- sadness
- fear
- surprise
- neutral

Notes:
- Annotate emotional transitions when emotional polarity or emotional category changes.
- If the emotional state remains unchanged, use "na".
- Emotion intensity changes without categorical transition may also indicate a shift when clearly expressed.

---

## 3. Trigger of Emotion Shift

Annotate the linguistic device that potentially induces or signals emotional change.

Available trigger labels:
- opposite
- negation
- irony
- na

Notes:
- Multiple triggers may co-occur in one span.
- Use a list when multiple triggers appear.
- Annotate triggers even if no actual emotion shift occurs.

Examples:
- negation: “不开心”
- opposite: “从开心到失落”
- irony: “你烦我开心[鼓掌]”

---

## 4. Mechanism of Emotion Shift

Annotate the underlying discourse-level mechanism associated with the emotional expression.

Available mechanism labels:
- contingent
- frustration
- satiation
- na

Definitions:
- contingent: emotion changes due to external events, situations, or contextual outcomes
- frustration: emotion changes caused by blocked expectation, negation, conflict, or unmet goals
- satiation: emotion changes caused by excessive repetition, prolonged exposure, or emotional saturation

Notes:
- Mechanism reflects the underlying cause of emotional interpretation.
- Not all emotion spans contain a mechanism.
- Use "na" if not applicable.

---

# Annotation Principles

1. Each Emotion Span corresponds to one annotation record.

2. All fields must be filled.
- Use "na" when a field is not applicable.

3. Keep spans minimal and continuous.

4. Keep the output format strictly consistent.
